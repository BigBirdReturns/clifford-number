# Redaction policy

This repo uses public-role-only graphing.

## Excluded data

The following categories do not belong in the public graph:

1. Private email addresses, phone numbers, home locations, birthdays, assistants, authentication tokens, or emergency contacts.
2. Intimate questionnaire responses, dating or matchmaking data, sex-life answers, dietary restrictions, personal predictions, or private biographical disclosures.
3. Raw registrant records or copied internal files.
4. Psychological, clinical, or inner-state characterization of named people.
5. Per-person treatment of private individuals whose public role does not create a legitimate public-interest hook.

## Included data

The graph may include:

1. Public professional roles.
2. Official appointments, commissions, publications, and procurement records.
3. Publicly reported membership, listing, registration, or attendance states, if the state is labeled precisely.
4. Institutional relationships that can be sourced without private data.
5. Derived structural edges, if clearly marked as derived or judgment.

## The leak rule

A public-interest leak can be evidence of a structure without becoming a license to republish private data. The graph maps the seam, not the personnel file.

## The status rule

Use the weakest accurate verb. Prefer `listed` over `attended` unless the source specifically supports attendance. Prefer `reported` over `confirmed` unless an official source or primary document supports the claim.

## The person rule

Do not make people the story when the institutional mechanism is enough. The memorable frame is the topology, not the private person.
